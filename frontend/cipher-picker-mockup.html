Cipher suite

A classical-only suite is permitted but no longer recommended. The store-now-decrypt-later threat applies to anything sent here.

Random number generator How nonces, IVs, and ephemeral keys are generated
FIPS-approved DRBG. (RNG choice is independent of the cipher tier.)
Key exchange How session keys are agreed
⚠ Classical ECDH is breakable by a sufficiently large quantum computer. Captured traffic can be decrypted retroactively.
Hash & HKDF How key material is derived
SHA-256 is unbroken but pairs poorly with AES-256 (128-bit collision margin vs. 256-bit cipher).
Symmetric encryption How file bytes & TLS records are sealed
⚠ AES-128 has a Grover-reduced effective security of 64 bits against a quantum adversary. Acceptable for short-lived secrets, not for archival.
Digital signature How sender identity is proven
⚠ ECDSA on 256-bit curves: forgeable by Shor's algorithm in polynomial time once large quantum computers exist.
